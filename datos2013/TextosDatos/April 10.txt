

==Events==
* 428 &ndash; Nestorius becomes Patriarch of Constantinople.
* 837 &ndash; Halley's Comet and Earth experienced their closest approach to one another when their separating distance equalled 0.0342 AU (3.2 million miles).
* 879 &ndash; Louis III becomes King of the Western Franks.
*1407 &ndash; the lama Deshin Shekpa visits the Ming Dynasty capital at Nanjing. He is awarded with the title Great Treasure Prince of Dharma.
*1500 &ndash; Ludovico Sforza is captured by the Swiss troops at Novara and is handed over to the French.
*1606 &ndash; The Charter of the Virginia Company of London is established by royal charter by James I of England with the purpose of establishing colonial settlements in North America.
*1710 &ndash; The Statute of Anne, the first law regulating copyright, enters into force in Great Britain.
*1741 &ndash; War of the Austrian Succession: Prussia defeats Austria in the Battle of Mollwitz.
*1809 &ndash; Napoleonic Wars: The War of the Fifth Coalition begins when Austria invades Bavaria.
*1815 &ndash; The Mount Tambora volcano begins a three-month-long eruption, lasting until July 15. The eruption ultimately kills 71,000 people and affects Earth's climate for the next two years.
*1816 &ndash; The United States Government approves the creation of the Second Bank of the United States.
*1821 &ndash; Patriarch Gregory V of Constantinople is hanged by the Ottoman government from the main gate of the Patriarchate and his body is thrown into the Bosphorus.
*1826 &ndash; The 10,500 inhabitants of the Greek town Missolonghi start leaving the town after a year's siege by Turkish forces. Very few of them survive.
*1856 &ndash; The Theta Chi fraternity is founded at Norwich University.
*1858 &ndash; After the original Big Ben, a 14.5 tonne bell for the Palace of Westminster had cracked during testing, it is recast into the current 13.76 tonne bell by Whitechapel Bell Foundry.
*1864 &ndash; Archduke Maximilian of Habsburg is proclaimed emperor of Mexico during the French intervention in Mexico.
*1865 &ndash; American Civil War: A day after his surrender to Union forces, Confederate General Robert E. Lee addresses his troops for the last time.
*1866 &ndash; The American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (ASPCA) is founded in New York City by Henry Bergh.
*1868 &ndash; At Arogee in Abyssinia, British and Indian forces defeat an army of Emperor Tewodros II. While 700 Ethiopians are killed and many more injured, only two die from the British/Indian troops.
*1874 &ndash; The first Arbor Day is celebrated in Nebraska.
*1887 &ndash; On Easter Sunday, Pope Leo XIII authorizes the establishment of The Catholic University of America.
*1904 &ndash; British mystic Aleister Crowley transcribes the third and final chapter of The Book of The Law.
*1912 &ndash; The Titanic leaves port in Southampton, England for her first and only voyage.
*1916 &ndash; The Professional Golfers Association of America (PGA) is created in New York City.
*1919 &ndash; Mexican Revolution leader Emiliano Zapata is ambushed and shot dead by government forces in Morelos.
*1925 &ndash; The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald is first published in New York City, by Charles Scribner's Sons.
*1941 &ndash; World War II: The Axis Powers in Europe establish the Independent State of Croatia from occupied Yugoslavia with Ante Pavelić's Ustaše fascist insurgents in power.
*1944 &ndash; Rudolf Vrba and Alfred Wetzler escape from the Birkenau death camp.
*1953 &ndash; Warner Brothers premieres the first 3-D film from a major American studio, entitled House of Wax.
*1957 &ndash; The Suez Canal is reopened for all shipping after being closed for three months.
*1959 &ndash; Akihito, future Emperor of Japan, weds Michiko.
*1963 &ndash; 129 American sailors die when the submarine USS Thresher sinks at sea.
*1968 &ndash; Shipwreck of the New Zealand inter-island ferry TEV Wahine outside Wellington harbour.
*1970 &ndash; Paul McCartney announces that he is leaving The Beatles for personal and professional reasons.
*1971 &ndash; Ping Pong Diplomacy: In an attempt to thaw relations with the United States, the People's Republic of China hosts the U.S. table tennis team for a weeklong visit.
*1972 &ndash; 20 days after he is kidnapped in Buenos Aires, Oberdan Sallustro is murdered by communist guerrillas.
* 1972 &ndash; Vietnam War: For the first time since November 1967, American B-52 bombers reportedly begin bombing North Vietnam.
* 1972 &ndash; Seventy-four nations sign the Biological Weapons Convention, the first multilateral disarmament treaty banning the production of biological weapons.
*1973 &ndash; A British Vanguard turboprop crashes during a snowstorm at Basel, Switzerland killing 104.
*1979 &ndash; Red River Valley Tornado Outbreak: A tornado lands in Wichita Falls, Texas killing 42 people.
*1991 &ndash; Italian ferry Moby Prince collides with an oil tanker in dense fog off Livorno, Italy killing 140.
* 1991 &ndash; A rare tropical storm develops in the South Atlantic Ocean near Angola; the first to be documented by satellites.
*1992 &ndash; The Maraghar Massacre, killing of ethnic Armenian civil population of the village Maraghar by Azerbaijani troops during the Nagorno-Karabakh War.
*2009 &ndash; President of Fiji Ratu Josefa Iloilo announces he will suspend the constitution and assume all governance in the country, creating a constitutional crisis.
*2010 &ndash; Polish Air Force Tu-154M crashes near Smolensk, Russia, killing all 96 people on board including President Lech Kaczyński.
