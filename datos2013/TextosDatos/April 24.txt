

==Events==
*1479 BC &ndash; Thutmose III ascends to the throne of Egypt, although power effectively shifts to Hatshepsut (according to the Low Chronology of the 18th Dynasty).
*1184 BC &ndash; Traditional date of the fall of Troy. 
*1547 &ndash; Battle of Mühlberg. Duke of Alba, commanding Spanish-Imperial forces of Charles I of Spain, defeats the troops of Schmalkaldic League.
*1558 &ndash; Mary, Queen of Scots, marries the Dauphin of France, François, at Notre Dame de Paris.
*1704 &ndash; The first regular newspaper in the United States, the News-Letter, is published in Boston, Massachusetts.
*1800 &ndash; The United States Library of Congress is established when President John Adams signs legislation to appropriate $5,000 USD to purchase "such books as may be necessary for the use of Congress".
*1877 &ndash; Russo-Turkish War: Russian Empire declares war on Ottoman Empire.
*1885 &ndash; American sharpshooter Annie Oakley was hired by Nate Salsbury to be a part of Buffalo Bill's Wild West.
*1898 &ndash; The Spanish-American War: The United States declares war on Spain.
*1904 &ndash; The Lithuanian press ban is lifted after almost 40 years.
*1907 &ndash; Hersheypark, founded by Milton S. Hershey for the exclusive use of his employees, is opened.
* 1907 &ndash; Al Ahly was founded.
*1913 &ndash; The Woolworth Building skyscraper in New York City is opened.
*1915 &ndash; The arrest of 250 Armenian intellectuals and community leaders in Istanbul marks the beginning of the Armenian Genocide.
*1916 &ndash; Easter Rising: The Irish Republican Brotherhood led by nationalists Patrick Pearse, James Connolly, and Joseph Plunkett starts a rebellion in Ireland.
* 1916 &ndash; Ernest Shackleton and five men of the Imperial Trans-Antarctic Expedition launch a lifeboat from uninhabited Elephant Island in the Southern Ocean to organise a rescue for the ice-trapped ship .
*1918 &ndash; First tank-to-tank combat, at Villers-Bretonneux, France, when three British Mark IVs met three German A7Vs.
*1922 &ndash; The first segment of the Imperial Wireless Chain providing wireless telegraphy between Leafield in Oxfordshire, England, and Cairo, Egypt, comes into operation.
*1926 &ndash; The Treaty of Berlin is signed. Germany and the Soviet Union each pledge neutrality in the event of an attack on the other by a third party for the next five years.
*1932 &ndash; Benny Rothman leads the mass trespass of Kinder Scout, leading to substantial legal reforms in the United Kingdom.
*1933 &ndash; Nazi Germany begins its persecution of Jehovah's Witnesses by shutting down the Watch Tower Society office in Magdeburg.
*1953 &ndash; Winston Churchill is knighted by Queen Elizabeth II.
*1955 &ndash; The Bandung Conference ends: 29 non-aligned nations of Asia and Africa finish a meeting that condemns colonialism, racism, and the Cold War.
*1957 &ndash; Suez Crisis: The Suez Canal is reopened following the introduction of UNEF peacekeepers to the region.
*1963 &ndash; Marriage of HRH Princess Alexandra of Kent to the Hon Angus Ogilvy at Westminster Abbey in London.
*1965 &ndash; Civil war breaks out in the Dominican Republic when Colonel Francisco Caamaño, overthrows the triumvirate that had been in power since the coup d'état against Juan Bosch.
*1967 &ndash; Cosmonaut Vladimir Komarov dies in Soyuz 1 when its parachute fails to open. He is the first human to die during a space mission.
* 1967 &ndash; Vietnam War: American General William Westmoreland says in a news conference that the enemy had "gained support in the United States that gives him hope that he can win politically that which he cannot win militarily."
*1968 &ndash; Mauritius becomes a member state of the United Nations.
*1970 &ndash; The first Chinese satellite, Dong Fang Hong I, is launched.
* 1970 &ndash; The Gambia becomes a republic within the Commonwealth of Nations, with Dawda Jawara as the first President.
*1971 &ndash; Soyuz 10 docks with Salyut 1.
*1980 &ndash; Eight U.S. servicemen die in Operation Eagle Claw as they attempt to end the Iran hostage crisis.
*1990 &ndash; STS-31: The Hubble Space Telescope is launched from the Space Shuttle Discovery.
Shuttle mission STS-31 lifts off, carrying Hubble into orbit.
* 1990 &ndash; Gruinard Island, Scotland, is officially declared free of the anthrax disease after 48 years of quarantine.
*1993 &ndash; An IRA bomb devastates the Bishopsgate area of London.
*1996 &ndash; In the United States, the Antiterrorism and Effective Death Penalty Act of 1996 is introduced.
*2004 &ndash; The United States lifts economic sanctions imposed on Libya 18 years previously, as a reward for its cooperation in eliminating weapons of mass destruction.
*2005 &ndash; Cardinal Joseph Ratzinger is inaugurated as the 265th Pope of the Roman Catholic Church taking the name Pope Benedict XVI.
* 2005 &ndash; Snuppy, the world's first cloned dog, is born in South Korea.
*2006 &ndash; King Gyanendra of Nepal gives into the demands of protesters and restores the parliament that he dissolved in 2002.
